Notifiable Disease Infection Rates in Australia

Vectorborne Diseases

Vector-borne diseases are diseases that are caused by parasites, bacteria and viruses. They are transmitted by vectors which are living organisms, that ingest a blood meal from an infected host to a new host.

Vaccine Preventable Diseases

Vaccination against viral and bacterial diseases is integral to control communicable diseases. Smallpox and Polio are great examples of how vaccines can eradicate diseases around the world. Immunizations and completion of the full schedule through adulthood is also a big contributor to reducing the incidence of vaccine preventable diseases.

  • Diphtheria
  • Haemophilus influenzae type b
  • Influenza (laboratory confirmed)
  • Measles
  • Mumps
  • Pertussis
  • Pneumococcal disease (invasive)
  • Poliovirus infection
  • Rotavirus
  • Rubella
  • Rubella congenital
  • Tetanus
  • Varicella zoster (chickenpox)
  • Varicella zoster (shingles)
  • Varicella zoster (unspecified)

Zoonoses

A zoonosis is a disease or infection that has jumped from vertebrate animals to humans. The zoonotic pathogens may be viral, parasitic or bacterial and can be spread to humans through food, direct contact, the environment or water.

Bloodborne Diseases

Bloodborne diseases are caused by pathogens that are carried in and spread through contact with blood. Some common routes of infection are blood transfusions, mother to child during pregnancy and needles.

  • Hepatitis (NEC)
  • Hepatitis B (newly acquired)
  • Hepatitis B (unspecified)
  • Hepatitis C (newly acquired)
  • Hepatitis C (unspecified)
  • Hepatitis D

Sexually Transmitted Diseases

More than 30 different bacteria, viruses and parasites are known to be transmitted through sexual contact. Eight of these pathogens are linked to the greatest incidence of sexually transmitted disease. Of these 8 infections, 4 are currently curable: syphilis, gonorrhoea, chlamydia and trichomoniasis. (1) More than 1 million sexually transmitted infections (STIs) are acquired every day worldwide (2, 3).

Gastrointestinal Diseases

Gastrointestinal diseases are diseases/disorders of the digestive tract with symptoms that range from mild to serious.

Other Notifiable Diseases

Some other notifiable diseases that do not fall into the main categories are leionellosis, leprosy, Meningococcal, Tuberculosis and RSV.

Quarantinable Diseases

Quarantinable disease are any disease which are communicable and presents a risk of serious harm to public health and may require quarantine to prevent its spread. The only disease with a notification rate in the dataset is COVID-19 for 2020.
