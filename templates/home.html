Notifiable Disease Infection Rates in Australia

Sexually Transmitted Diseases

More than 30 different bacteria, viruses and parasites are known to be transmitted through sexual contact. Eight of these pathogens are linked to the greatest incidence of sexually transmitted disease. Of these 8 infections, 4 are currently curable: syphilis, gonorrhoea, chlamydia and trichomoniasis. More than 1 million sexually transmitted infections (STIs) are acquired every day worldwide.

Our Analysis Indicates:

The Northern Territory had the highest occurance of STIs across all years.

Chlamydia and Gonorrhoea were the most commenly occuring infections.

Explore Data
Preventative Measures:

Increase sexual health awareness and education especially in the Northern Territory.

Fund more sexual health clinics in remote areas for early identification and intervention.

Vaccine Preventable Diseases

Vaccination against viral and bacterial diseases is integral to control communicable diseases. Smallpox and Polio are great examples of how vaccines can eradicate diseases around the world. Immunizations and completion of the full schedule through adulthood is also a big contributor to reducing the incidence of vaccine preventable diseases.

Our Analysis Indicates:

Influenza is the highest occuring disease that is vaccine preventable. Spikes in infection rates occured in 2017 and 2019

In general all other diseases are decreasing in infection rate over time. The rate of Shingles infection remains steady through most states.

Explore Data
Preventative Measures:

Identify age groups experiencing higher rates of infections that are not participating in vaccine programs.

Increase availability of medicare funded vaccines to groups.

Gastrointestinal Diseases

Gastrointestinal diseases are diseases/disorders of the digestive tract with symptoms that range from mild to serious. They can be spread from person to person or through contaminated food and drinking water.

Our Analysis Indicates:

The Northern Territory had the highest rates of gastrointestinal disease, however there appears to be a decreasing trend over time.

In most states Campylobacteriosis and Salmonellosis were the two most commonly occuring infections. The Northern Territory had a distinctive elevated occurance of Botulism and Cryptosporidiosis.

Explore Data
Preventative Measures:

Increase education and awareness in food safety and food preparation hygiene.

Ensure access to adequate sanitation and safe drinking water in remote areas.

Bloodborne Diseases

Bloodborne diseases are caused by pathogens that are carried in and spread through contact with blood. Some common routes of infection are blood transfusions, mother to child during pregnancy and needles.

Our Analysis Indicates:

Bloodborne disease rates are similar across all states, with a slight elevation in the Northern Territory.

Hepatitis C and Hepatitis B are the most commonly occuring diseases in this group however both are decreasing in rate over time across all states.

Explore Data
Preventative Measures:

Education and awareness on safe usage and disposal of syringes. Provision of safe needle exchange centers.

Increase availability of medicare funded Hepatisis B vaccine.

Vectorborne Diseases

Vectorborne diseases are caused by parasites, bacteria and viruses. They are transmitted by vectors which are living organisms, that ingest a blood meal from an infected host to a new host.

Our Analysis Indicates:

Queensland and Northern Territory have the highest rates of vectorborne diseases.

The two most commonly occuring diseases are Ross River Virus and Dengue Fever. Both infection rates are decreasing over time.

Explore Data
Preventative Measures:

Identify age groups experiencing higher rates of infections that are not participating in vaccine programs.

Increase avialability of medicare funded vaccines to groups.

Zoonoses

A zoonosis is a disease or infection that has jumped from vertebrate animals to humans. The zoonotic pathogens may be viral, parasitic or bacterial and can be spread to humans through food, direct contact, the environment or water.

Our Analysis Indicates:

There is minimal risk of zoonosis in Australia

There had been a apparent spike of Leptospirosis in Queensland in 2017 however this has significantly reduced over time.

Explore Data
Preventative Measures:

Ensure adequate pest control in agricultural and residential areas.

Increase public awareness of risks during times of heavy rainfall/flooding.

Other Notifiable Diseases

Some other notifiable diseases that do not fall into the main categories are leionellosis, leprosy, Meningococcal, Tuberculosis and RSV.

Our Analysis Indicates:

There is a minimal number of records of other notifiable diseases.

Tuberculosis and Legionellosis are the two most commonly occuring diseases.

Explore Data
Preventative Measures:

An increase in identification and reporting of these diseases may give a better insight into their occurance in Australia.

Quarantinable Diseases

Quarantinable disease are any disease which are communicable and presents a risk of serious harm to public health and may require quarantine to prevent its spread.

Our Analysis Indicates:

The only disease with a notification rate in the dataset is COVID-19 for 2020.

Explore Data
Preventative Measures:

Australia is currently engaging in several measures to control COVID-19 including isolation of infectious and potentially infectious inviduals, physical distancing, increased hygiene, limitations on gatherings and mass vaccinations.
